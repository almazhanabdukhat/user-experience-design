Most common K-drama tropes

Hello! I am a big fan of k-dramas (doramas) and on this page I will be discussing the most common tropes that you might encounter in k-dramas :) Every good dorama has a few tropes here and there - sometimes we love them, sometimes we love to hate them and they are always fun and even somewhat comforting to watch...so, let's dive in!

Destined love.

Characters meet and fall in love per usual, but then, turns out, they already had a very significant connection in the past and forgot about it (i.e. they were raised in the same orphanage or their family members were/are enemies or even worse - dated). Usually, small hints are given throughout the series and the big revelation happens to the end of the series. Commonly this trope is employed when there is a need to spice things up.

Amnesia

Main characters are living happily and then, due to some unfortunate events, one character experiences memory loss (either due to accident or medical trauma). Of course, amnesia is a serious illness and is not a topic for jokes. However, in k-dramas amnesia occurs too more often and works as a very effective plot to create suspense. Commonly, in spite of the memory loss, main characters still fall in love once again or their memory suddenly recovers due to a very fortunate sequence of events. Amnesia trope is a powerful way to emphasize that some couples will be together no matter what.

Stalking second lead.

In k-dramas, stalking is quite common for both genders - it can be both cute and a bit disturbing if start analyzing all the psychological implications. In k-dramas, stalking is used to add some fun and cuteness to the drama and show how attractive the object of the pursuit is. Try clicking on images to simulate stalking for a bit.
